# Pricing Models — How to Price ROI360 Storefronts

## Core Principle

**Never give the storefront away to win print.** The platform has real value — it saves your client time, reduces errors, speeds up ordering, and gives them brand control. Price it accordingly.

If you give it away free, your client won't value it, won't use it, and you'll be supporting a platform that generates no revenue.

## Pricing Approaches

### 1. Monthly Platform Fee
- Charge a fixed monthly fee for access to the storefront
- Typically £200–£1,000/month depending on complexity, number of users, and templates
- Good for: clients who want predictability, ongoing relationship
- Example: "£495/month includes platform access, 10 templates, admin dashboard, and support"

### 2. Setup + Monthly Fee
- One-off setup fee (covers template building, configuration, training) + ongoing monthly
- Setup: £1,000–£5,000+ depending on scope
- Monthly: £200–£500
- Good for: larger implementations with significant upfront work

### 3. Per-Template Pricing
- Charge per template created and maintained
- Typically £50–£150 per template per month, or a one-off build cost of £200–£500 per template
- Good for: clients who want to start small and add templates over time

### 4. Included in Print Contract
- Build the platform cost into your print pricing
- Add a margin to print orders that flow through the storefront
- Good for: clients with high print volumes where the storefront is the ordering mechanism
- **Caution:** Make sure the platform value is visible — don't let it become invisible or they'll expect it free if they move print elsewhere

### 5. Milestone-Based
- Charge based on milestones: setup, go-live, template additions, training sessions
- Good for: phased implementations where scope grows over time

### 6. Rebate Against Print
- Offer the platform at list price but rebate a portion against print spend
- Example: "Platform is £500/month, but we'll credit £200/month against your print orders over £2,000"
- Good for: incentivising print volume while still pricing the platform properly

## Pricing Don'ts

- **Don't give it away free** — ever. Even as a "trial." Charge something.
- **Don't price it as "just a website"** — it's a business tool, not a brochure site
- **Don't compete on price with Canva** — Canva is a design tool, ROI360 is a branded ordering and production platform with workflow, approvals, and print integration
- **Don't discount without a reason** — if you discount, tie it to commitment (multi-year, minimum spend, referral)
- **Don't let the client dictate pricing** — you're the expert, you know the value

## Pricing the Proof of Concept (POC)

- **Always charge for a POC** — even if it's a reduced rate
- Typical POC: 3-5 templates, 3-6 months, full list price or small discount
- Define success criteria upfront: "If X users are ordering Y materials per month after 3 months, we expand"
- POC should demonstrate value quickly — pick high-volume, high-pain templates

## How to Talk About Price

Instead of leading with a number, lead with the problem and the cost of NOT solving it:

- "How much time does your team spend requesting, chasing, and checking marketing materials each month?"
- "What does a 2-day turnaround on a business card change actually cost you in staff time?"
- "If your designers are spending 40% of their time on repetitive amendments, what's that worth to free up?"
- "One off-brand leaflet going out to 10,000 customers — what's the reputational risk worth?"

Then frame the investment:
- "For less than the cost of one designer day per month, your entire team gets self-service access to brand-perfect materials, 24/7"
