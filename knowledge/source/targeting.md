# Targeting — Who Is a Good Fit for ROI360?

## Overview

ROI360 is a web-to-print platform built on PageFlex Storefront technology. It enables print businesses and agencies to offer their clients branded, self-service ordering portals for marketing materials, business stationery, signage, and more. The platform is sold exclusively through reseller partners — predominantly printers evolving into marketing service providers.

## Good-Fit End Clients

The best clients for an ROI360 storefront are organisations that:

### Must-Haves
- **Produce recurring branded materials** — not one-off projects but ongoing need for business cards, leaflets, posters, banners, stationery, signage, POS, social media templates
- **Have multiple locations, branches, or users** — the more people who need to order/create materials, the higher the value (franchises, multi-site businesses, distributed sales teams)
- **Currently rely on email/phone ordering** — sending briefs to a designer or studio, waiting days for turnaround, manual amendments
- **Have brand consistency challenges** — local teams going off-brand, using outdated logos, creating their own materials in Word/PowerPoint/Canva

### Strong Indicators
- **Franchise networks** (20+ locations) — franchisees need local marketing but HQ needs brand control
- **Multi-site retail, hospitality, or care businesses** — each site needs localised materials (menus, rosters, posters, notices)
- **Field sales organisations** — reps need to customise proposals, one-pagers, leave-behinds on the go
- **Organisations with a print contract** — already spending with you on print; the storefront becomes the ordering mechanism
- **Growing businesses** — adding locations, hiring, launching new products = more materials needed
- **Organisations with compliance requirements** — regulated industries (care, finance, legal) where materials must contain current terms and approved content

### Industries That Work Well
- Print and marketing services providers (your fellow partners)
- Franchise networks (food, care, retail, automotive, property)
- Multi-site hospitality (hotels, pub groups, restaurant chains)
- Care providers (domiciliary care, care homes, NHS suppliers)
- Estate agents and property groups
- Construction and trades (multi-branch merchants, hire companies)
- Charities and not-for-profits with regional offices
- Education (multi-academy trusts, university departments)
- Financial services (IFA networks, insurance brokers)

## Red Flags — Poor Fit
- **Tiny organisations** — fewer than 3 locations or under 20 users; not enough volume to justify the platform
- **One-off project mindset** — looking for a single campaign, not an ongoing platform
- **No print relationship** — cold prospects with no existing connection to your print business
- **DIY culture** — small businesses where one person does everything in Canva and is happy with it
- **No brand guidelines** — if they don't have a brand to protect, there's less value in a controlled platform
- **Highly technical/custom needs** — complex variable data or integration requirements that exceed standard PageFlex capabilities without custom development

## Qualification Questions
1. How many locations/branches/users do you have?
2. What branded materials do you produce regularly?
3. How do people currently order or create these materials?
4. What's your biggest frustration with the current process?
5. Do you have brand guidelines that need to be followed?
6. Who currently manages the creation and approval of materials?
7. How much do you spend on design/agency fees for routine materials?
8. Are you an existing print customer? What do you currently order?
