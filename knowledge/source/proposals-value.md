# Proposals & Value Stories — Building Compelling Cases

## The Value Story Structure

Every proposal should follow this structure. Don't lead with features — lead with the client's problem.

### 1. The Problem (Their World Today)
Describe what you learned in discovery. Use their words. Make it specific.

**Example:** "Your 45 branch managers currently email the marketing team when they need updated leaflets. The average turnaround is 5 working days. Half the requests need amendments. Meanwhile, some branches are creating their own materials in Word, which are off-brand and sometimes contain outdated pricing."

### 2. The Impact (What It's Costing Them)
Quantify wherever possible — time, money, risk.

**Example:** "Based on our conversation, we estimate your team spends approximately 15 hours per week managing material requests and amendments. That's nearly £20,000 per year in staff time alone — before accounting for the cost of off-brand materials reaching customers or the opportunity cost of your designers doing repetitive work instead of strategic projects."

### 3. The Solution (How ROI360 Fixes It)
Connect the platform to the problem. Be specific about what changes.

**Example:** "With ROI360, your branch managers log in, select the leaflet they need, enter their branch details, preview it, and order — in under 5 minutes. The template is brand-locked so they can't go off-brand. Updated pricing is pulled from your central database automatically. If approval is needed, their regional manager gets a notification and can approve with one click."

### 4. The Proof (Evidence It Works)
Use proof points, case studies, or a POC.

**Example:** "Across our implementations, clients typically see a 70-80% reduction in material request turnaround time and near-elimination of off-brand materials. One client with 200+ locations went from 5-day turnaround to same-day self-service."

### 5. The Investment (What It Costs)
Frame as investment, not cost. Compare to the problem cost.

**Example:** "The platform investment is £495/month, including 10 templates, admin dashboard, user management, and ongoing support. That's less than 30% of the staff time you're currently spending on material management."

### 6. The Next Step (What Happens Now)
Make it easy and low-risk.

**Example:** "I suggest we start with a 3-month proof of concept: we'll set up 5 of your highest-volume templates, train your team, and measure the impact. If it delivers the results we've outlined, we expand from there."

## ROI Calculation Framework

Use this to build a quick ROI narrative for any client:

### Time Savings
- Current requests per week: ___
- Average time per request (including amendments): ___ hours
- Staff hourly cost (loaded): £___
- **Annual cost of current process:** requests × hours × cost × 52

### Design/Agency Savings
- Current monthly spend on routine design: £___
- Percentage that could be templated: ___%
- **Annual savings potential:** monthly spend × % × 12

### Error/Risk Reduction
- Estimated off-brand materials per year: ___
- Cost per incident (reprint, customer impact, compliance): £___
- **Annual risk reduction:** incidents × cost

### Total ROI
- Annual savings: £___
- Platform investment: £___/year
- **Net annual benefit:** savings minus investment
- **Payback period:** investment ÷ (savings ÷ 12) months
