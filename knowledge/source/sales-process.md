# Sales Process — How to Sell ROI360 Storefronts

## The Fitness Programme Philosophy

Selling ROI360 is not about selling software. It's about solving a business problem: your client wastes time, money, and brand consistency because their people can't easily get the branded materials they need. ROI360 is the solution — but you need to uncover the problem first.

**Consultative, not transactional.** Ask questions. Listen. Understand their world before you pitch anything.

## Sales Stages

### 1. Identify (Who to approach?)
- Start with your **existing print customers** — they already trust you and spend money with you
- Look for clients who order the same types of materials repeatedly (business cards, leaflets, posters, banners)
- Look for clients with multiple sites, branches, or a distributed team
- Ask: "Who in your business orders printed materials? How many people are involved?"

### 2. Discovery (What's the problem?)
- **Find out where it hurts.** The goal is to understand their current process and its pain points
- Key discovery questions:
  - "Walk me through what happens when someone in your business needs a new business card / leaflet / poster"
  - "How long does that typically take from request to delivered product?"
  - "Who's involved in the process? How many emails or touch points?"
  - "What happens when someone uses an old logo or creates something off-brand?"
  - "How do you currently control what gets produced?"
  - "What does your design/agency spend look like for routine materials?"
  - "If you could change one thing about how your team gets marketing materials, what would it be?"

### 3. Demonstrate (Show the solution)
- **Always demo with their materials in mind** — don't show generic templates
- If possible, build a simple proof-of-concept with their logo, colours, and one or two templates
- Show the user journey: select template → customise → order → approve → produce
- Emphasise: speed (minutes not days), brand control (locked elements), self-service (24/7)
- Show the admin view: who's ordering what, approval workflows, template management

### 4. Propose (Make it real)
- Frame the proposal around the problem you discovered, not features
- Structure: Problem → Solution → How it works → Investment → Next steps
- Include a proof-of-concept phase — don't try to boil the ocean on day one
- Suggest starting with 3-5 high-volume templates that will have immediate impact

### 5. Close (Get commitment)
- Address objections directly (see objections.md)
- Create urgency: "How many more weeks of the current process are you willing to accept?"
- Make the first step small: "Let's start with a pilot — 5 templates, 3 months, see the results"

### 6. Implement & Grow
- Successful onboarding leads to expansion — more templates, more users, more products
- Regular check-ins to review usage, identify new template opportunities, and demonstrate value

## Who to Target in the Organisation

| Role | Why They Care | How to Approach |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Marketing Manager / Brand Manager** | Brand consistency, speed, reducing design bottleneck | Lead with "empower your team without losing control" |
| **Operations Director / COO** | Efficiency, cost reduction, process standardisation | Lead with "reduce the time and cost of getting materials to your people" |
| **Franchise Director** | Franchisee compliance, engagement, speed | Lead with "give franchisees what they need in minutes, with your brand locked in" |
| **Business Owner / MD** | Cost, efficiency, competitive advantage | Lead with "add a service your competitors don't offer" — if selling to a printer |
| **Purchasing / Procurement** | Spend control, approved suppliers, audit trail | Lead with "centralise ordering, control spend, track everything" |

## Key Messages

- "Your team gets what they need in minutes, not days"
- "Your brand stays consistent every time, automatically"
- "You can see who's ordering what, approve what needs approval, and track everything"
- "It's not about replacing your designer — it's about freeing them from repetitive work"
- "Start small, prove the value, then grow"
