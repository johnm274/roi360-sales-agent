# Objection Handling — Common Pushbacks and How to Respond

## "We don't have time for this right now"

**Response:** "I completely understand — you're busy. That's actually the point. How much time is your team spending right now on requesting, chasing, and checking marketing materials? The platform saves that time. And we can start small — 3-5 templates, up and running in 4-6 weeks. The sooner we start, the sooner your team gets their time back."

## "We already use Canva / SharePoint / Word templates"

**Response:** "Those are great tools for individual design work. But they don't give you brand control at scale, approval workflows, print integration, or admin visibility into who's ordering what. ROI360 isn't a design tool — it's a branded ordering platform that connects to your print production. Your team gets self-service. You get control and a direct route to print orders."

## "It's too expensive / we don't have the budget"

**Response:** "Let me ask — what's your current spend on design amendments, agency fees, and staff time managing material requests? Most of our clients find the platform pays for itself within 2-3 months by eliminating that overhead. And we can start with a focused pilot to prove the ROI before expanding."

**Follow-up:** "What would it be worth to you if every branch could get the materials they need in 5 minutes instead of 5 days?"

## "Our team won't use it"

**Response:** "That's a fair concern. Here's what we've found: when the platform is easier than the current process — and it always is — adoption follows. We start with the highest-pain, highest-volume templates so the first experience is 'wow, that was quick.' We also provide training and can track adoption so we know exactly who's using it and who needs support."

## "We tried something like this before and it didn't work"

**Response:** "What happened? Often the issue is that the first attempt was too ambitious — too many templates, not enough training, or it wasn't tied to how people actually work. We take a different approach: start small, prove value fast, then expand. A focused pilot with 3-5 templates avoids the mistakes of doing too much too soon."

## "Can we just have a basic website / PDF download?"

**Response:** "We could, but that misses the real value. A basic website doesn't let users customise materials with their details, doesn't integrate with print production, doesn't give you approval workflows, and doesn't show you who's using what. The ROI360 storefront is a working tool, not a catalogue."

## "We're too small for this"

**Response:** "Actually, smaller businesses often see the biggest impact because they don't have a dedicated marketing team. If you've got even 5-10 people who need branded materials, the platform removes the bottleneck of everything going through one person. And the investment scales — we can start at a level that fits."

## "We need to talk to IT / we have security concerns"

**Response:** "Of course. The platform is cloud-hosted, secure, and GDPR compliant. We can provide technical documentation, security information, and connect your IT team with our technical lead. Most implementations don't require any IT involvement beyond SSO setup if that's needed."

## "What happens if we stop using it?"

**Response:** "Your templates and data remain accessible during your contract period. If you decide to leave, we'll help with a clean handover. But honestly, once teams are using it and seeing the speed and quality, they don't go back to email and PDFs."

## "Why ROI360 and not [competitor]?"

**Response:** "Great question. What are you comparing us to? [Listen first.] ROI360 is built on PageFlex — one of the most established web-to-print platforms globally. What makes us different is: we're a partner-delivered service, so you get local support and someone who understands your business. Plus, the platform is deeply customisable — we can match your client's exact workflow, not force them into a rigid template."

## "Can we see a demo first?"

**Response:** "Absolutely. Even better, let me show you with your own materials. If you can share your logo, brand colours, and one or two items you produce regularly, I'll set up a preview so you can see exactly what your team would experience."
